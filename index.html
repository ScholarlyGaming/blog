Projects

Check out my Clash Royale Engine, made from the ground up in Khan Academy in fall/early winter 2019 and ported to Code Sandbox in late winter/spring 2020.

It's a highly technical project - Clash Royale pretty much runs entirely on Supercell's servers, and having no affiliation with Supercell, I have no knowledge about the server's inner workings. This made it difficult to emulate the game, as I had to try to replicate the results as close as possible.

In the end, however, I was able to replicate the game with fairly high accuracy, and it was able to parse a replay from the official game and get very close to the actual gameplay.

Or look at my original game Project Alana, a 40-level isometric platformer made in spring 2019. With custom graphics and cutscenes as well as a focus on a story, this is definitely my favorite game that I've built.

The gameplay focuses on using the character's tight controls and a set of limited abilities to navigate different obstacles like moving blocks, reverse gravity fields, ice, and a variety of enemies.
